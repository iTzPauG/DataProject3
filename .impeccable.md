## Design Context

### Users
Locals and visitors in Valencia (Spain) opening the app on the street — one hand, often in bright sunlight or at dusk. They are deciding *where to go next* in the next 30 minutes: a meal, a late drink, a pharmacy, a market, an incident to avoid. The job is **orient, pick, go** — not browse, not discover-by-inspiration. Short sessions, impatient thumbs.

### Brand Personality
Local, assured, rooted. The app reads the city on the user's behalf — it should feel like a well-travelled friend who lowers their voice and points, not a neon kiosk shouting offers. Three words: **grounded · editorial · quietly confident**.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial dark. Think of a printed city companion — *Monocle* travel guides, *Kinfolk* maps, *Arc Browser* sidebars, *Raycast* density — more than SaaS product pages. Primary surface is a deep warm-tinted charcoal (dusk, not midnight). One restrained indigo accent used for affordances only. Category identity comes from **monograms inside hairline rings**, not colored emoji in rounded squares. Typography does the hierarchy; icons only where icons pay for themselves.

**Anti-references**: generic "Spotify-dark + purple gradient" SaaS template, colorful emoji-in-chip icons, glass-morphism floating panels, saturated category color blobs, Material-ish elevated cards with heavy drop shadow.

### Design Principles
1. **Typography carries the hierarchy.** Two type families, one display-editorial, one neutral-utility. Sizes vary by ≥1.25×.
2. **No user-visible emoji glyphs.** Iconography is monochrome geometry (hairline circles, glyphs drawn with View primitives) or monogram letters. Color on icons is reserved for status (read / unread / warning).
3. **One accent, used rarely.** The brand indigo is for active-state, primary CTA, and link — nothing else. Everything else lives on the neutral ladder.
4. **Hairline rules, not elevated cards.** Section boundaries are 1px dividers tinted with the brand hue; avoid shadowed containers stacked into a grid.
5. **Asymmetric, left-aligned.** Titles bleed to the left edge with generous top-margin. No centered screens, no identical-sized grids.
